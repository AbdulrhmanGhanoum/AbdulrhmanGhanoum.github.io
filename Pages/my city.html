saudi arabia

Saudi Arabia, officially known as the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia, is a country located on the Arabian Peninsula and is the largest country in the Middle East by land area. The kingdom was founded in 1932 by King Abdulaziz Al Saud. Saudi Arabia is distinguished by its spiritual significance to Muslims, as it is home to the holiest cities in Islam: Mecca and Medina.

Geography and Climate

Saudi Arabia covers an area of approximately 2,150,000 square kilometers, bordered by Jordan and Iraq to the north, Kuwait to the northeast, Bahrain, Qatar, and the United Arab Emirates to the east, Oman to the southeast, and Yemen to the south. The country has a coastline along the Red Sea to the west and the Arabian Gulf to the east. Its climate is generally hot and dry, with climatic variations across different regions.

Population and Culture

Saudi Arabia has a population of around 34 million people, with Saudis comprising the vast majority. Arabic is the official language, and Islam is the official religion. The country boasts a rich culture and a long history spanning thousands of years. Saudi Arabia maintains its cultural and religious traditions while embracing modernization and development.

jeddah

Jeddah is a bustling coastal city in Saudi Arabia, known for its vibrant culture, historic significance, and economic importance. As the gateway to Mecca, it holds immense religious significance for Muslims worldwide. Jeddah boasts a diverse population and a thriving economy, with a bustling seaport and a growing skyline marked by modern skyscrapers. Its old town, Al-Balad, features traditional coral architecture and historic souks, offering a glimpse into the city's rich heritage. Jeddah is also famous for its waterfront Corniche, lined with parks, restaurants, and recreational facilities, making it a popular destination for locals and tourists alike.
